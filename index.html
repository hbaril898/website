    • Understanding Endangered Species

    Animals, plants, or other organisms that face extinction as a result of declining populations or habitat destruction are known as endangered species. Deforestation, pollution, poaching, and climate change are some of the causes of this catastrophe. Because these species are vital to preserving ecosystem balance, protecting them is a top priority on a worldwide scale. The goal of conservation is to keep them from going extinct, emphasizing how urgent it is to protect biodiversity.

    Biodiversity

    By preserving natural resources, pollination, and food chains, biodiversity keeps ecosystems stable. There are environmental repercussions when a species becomes extinct or becomes endangered. The interdependence of all life on Earth is highlighted by the fact that the extinction of a single species can upend entire ecosystems. Future generations' well-being and environmental resilience are guaranteed when endangered species are protected.
